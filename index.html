Featured exhibits

Our rolling stock hall preserves vehicles from three different eras of city and regional transit. All three cars below are on permanent static display.

Lego City Electric No. 717 on display in the Rolling Stock Hall.

Exhibit 01 · Streetcar Era

Lego City Electric — Hollywood Car No. 717

Built in the 1920s for city streetcar service, No. 717 is one of the last operable "Hollywood" class cars from the original Lego City Electric network. Riders once boarded cars just like this one to travel from downtown out to the Hollywood Boulevard line — long before the county's rail lines went underground.

  • Built1925
  • Class600-Series "Hollywood"
  • Service ended1956
  • StatusStatic display

A 42nd Street Shuttle car, preserved from the county's original shuttle fleet.

Exhibit 02 · Subway Era

42nd Street Shuttle Car

This shuttle car ran the short, high-frequency route connecting two of the county's busiest downtown transfer points. Built for quick turnarounds and heavy rush-hour crowds, the shuttle fleet became one of the most recognizable — and shortest — rides in the whole system.

  • RouteDowntown Shuttle
  • Car length51 ft
  • ConfigurationMarried pair
  • StatusStatic display

A preserved 9 train car, formerly of the skip-stop local fleet.

Exhibit 03 · Subway Era

The 9 Train

During rush hour, the 9 ran a skip-stop pattern alongside its sister route, shaving minutes off the commute for riders headed toward the northern reaches of the county. Retired when ridership patterns changed, the 9 remains a favorite of longtime rail fans.

  • Service typeRush-hour skip-stop
  • Bullet colorRed
  • Retired2005
  • StatusStatic display
